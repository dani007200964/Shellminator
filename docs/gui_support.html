Why Use a Terminal Interface in 2024?

When people hear command-line interface, many start raising their eyebrows. Sure, it's not the newest invention—but hey, neither is the wheel, and that still works pretty well!

Most modern interfaces are graphical, and for good reason—they’re designed to make life easier for the average user. But when it comes to developer tools, the best solutions are almost always terminal-based, either partially or entirely.

What many people don’t realize is that, even though terminals are text-based by nature, you can actually add graphical elements to them. Shellminator does this using VT100 sequences, a feature that’s been around for ages and is supported by pretty much every modern terminal emulator.

But Why Add GUI Features to a Terminal?

No, we’re not suggesting you build the next AAA video game in a terminal. But for system diagnostics and real-time monitoring? Absolutely!

A simple number on the screen is fine, but let’s be real—your brain processes visual information much faster. A progress bar, a button, or a status indicator makes things way more intuitive.

With Shellminator, you can create:

  • Buttons
  • Progress bars
  • Notifications
  • Selection lists
  • Level meters
  • Plots

…and even combine them into a fully interactive layout. So if you’re ready to take your terminal-based system to the next level, you’re in the right place!
